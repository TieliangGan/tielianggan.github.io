Publications

G-quadruplex inducer/stabilizer pyridostatin targets SUB1 to promote cytotoxicity of a transplatinum complex
Contributors: Yinzhu Hou; Tieliang Gan; Tiantian Fang; Yao Zhao; Qun Luo; Xingkai Liu; Luyu Qi; Yanyan Zhang; Feifei Jia; Juanjuan Han et al.
Nucleic Acids Research (April 8th, 2022)
DOI: 10.1093/nar/gkac151

Pyridostatin (PDS) is a well-known G-quadruplex (G4) inducer and stabilizer, yet its target genes have remained unclear. Herein, applying MS proteomics strategy, we revealed PDS signiﬁcantly downregulated 22 proteins but upregulated 16 proteins in HeLa cancer cells, of which the genes both contain a number of G4 potential sequences, implying that PDS regulation on gene expression is far more complicated than inducing/stabilizing G4 structures. The PDS-downregulated proteins consequently upregulated 6 proteins to activate cyclin and cell cycle regulation, suggesting that PDS itself is not a potential anticancer agent, at least toward HeLa cancer cells. Importantly, SUB1, which encodes human positive cofactor and DNA lesion sensor PC4, was downregulated by 4.76-fold. Further studies demonstrated that the downregulation of PC4 dramatically promoted the cytotoxicity of trans-[PtCl2 (NH3 )(thiazole)] (trans-PtTz) toward HeLa cells to a similar level of cisplatin, contributable to retarding the repair of 1,3-trans-PtTz crosslinked DNA lesion mediated by PC4. These ﬁndings not only provide new insights into better understanding on the biological functions of PDS but also implicate a strategy for the rational design of novel multi-targeting platinum anticancer drugs via conjugation of PDS as a ligand to the coordination scaffold of transplatin for battling drug resistance to cisplatin.

The Potential Autophagic Role of Caspase 6, Caspase 8 and Caspase 14

Feb 2023 - Present (ongoing)

The previous data of our lab shows the function of one of the two zinc finger domains of the autophagy receptor T6BP, the T6BP (725-753) still remains elusive. The mass spectrometry data of T6BP, at the same time, shows significant enrichment of caspase 6, caspase 8 and caspase 14. Recent study reveals the potential involvement of T6BP in a newly-discovered anti-apoptosis process as another checkpoint together with caspase 8, FIP200 and ATG9A. Considering the important role of T6BP in autophagy, in combination with our previous data, we speculate that caspase 8, as well as other caspases, may play a role in autophagy. My project is to explore the molecular and cellular mechanisms of these potential interactions.

This project will be divided into 3 parallel parts, which are computational part, biochemical part and cellular biological part.

I am still in the very first stage of this project. In order to validate our presumptions, I will first use ColabFold on our server to predict the possible structure of the protein complex. Then I will use E. coli to express the procaspase 6, procaspase 8 and their different domains, as well as the zinc finger domains of T6BP. We will use FPLC as the first step to examine the interactions between these domains. In vitro reconstruction of functional caspases will also be conducted. If the interactions do exist, further biochemical characterizations including SEC-MALS, ITC, AUC and so on will be conducted. Protein complex will be reconstructed in vitro to solve the structure by X-ray crystallography or cryo-EM.

If proven feasible, I will do the cell biology experiments to further elucidate the mechanism of the cross talk. In the process of the experiments, the hypothesis is subject to change.

Coming soon

This project is carried out by myself, at Shanghai Institute of Orgranic Chemistry, Chinese Academy of Sciences, under the supervision of Lifeng Pan.


Characterization of the Potential Autophagy Receptor Protein 53BP1

Sep 2022 - Present (ongoing)

53BP1 is mostly well-known for its role in DNA Damage Response. However, in our study of the autophagy receptor T6BP, the mass spectrometry data shows significant enrichment of 53BP1, which leads us to postulate other role of this protein. Recent study from other groups shows that other than the classic role of DNA repair in NHEJ, 53BP1 can also function as regulator of heterochromatin and phase separationand is involved in mitophagy. Based on these results, we seek to further illustrate the potential role and mechanism of 53BP1 in autophagy. Furthermore, the possibility of autophagy happening in nucleus is also an elusive part in this field.

As a potential autophagy receptor, I first examined its interaction with the claw domain of FIP200 and with the six proteins in ATG8 family. Then the ITC, AUC and SEC-MALS experiments were carried out to further characterize the biochemical properties of these interactions. At the same time, crystal screen was conducted and the related bioinformatic analysis was also done.

Co-localization of full length 53BP1 and other related proteins under different conditions is on the way, in combination with other characterization tools such as co-IP, FP, and so on. The hypothesis is subject to change as my experiments are carried out further and further.

I have already processed data from FPLC, ITC, AUC and SEC-MALS. The first round of co-localization was done and the crystal screen is still pending for results. Cell biology assay is on the way.

This project is carried out by myself, at Shanghai Institute of Orgranic Chemistry, Chinese Academy of Sciences, under the supervision of Lifeng Pan.


The Cytotoxicity of a trans-platinum Complex in Combination with PDS on HeLa Cells

Sep 2020 - Mar 2021

Based on the previous study of the group, pyridostatin (PDS) is able to downregulate the expression of PC4 protein (encoded by SUB1) and stabilize DNA-quadraplex (G4). As a response for DNA damage caused by platinum complex, PC4 protein can bind to the DNA-Platinum complex to rescue the cell from death. Based on these results, we postulate that under the condition of PDS, cells can be more vulnerable to the platinum complex trans-PtTz, thus increase the cytotoxicity of the compound, making it, aside from cisplatin, worth further function as a compound against cancer.

I first synthesized the compound trans-PtTz, which has two thiazole ring on the trans position. The structure of the molecule was validated by MALDI-MS and NMR. Then, by applying trans-PtTz to HeLa cell culture under the condition of different concentration of PDS, the IC50 of trans-PtTz under this condition, as well as the control group, were determined. Further analysis confirm the downregulation of the gene SUB1 by MS proteomics.

The result was published on Nucleic Acids Research.

The cytotoxicity of trans-PtTz is significantly increased to the level comparable to the traditional cisplatin. The results can be seen on:

Characterization of Different Human Body Fluids

Jun 2019 - Jan 2021

In the realm of forensic investigation, it is critical to accurately differentiate among various bodily fluids. Six specific fluids, including semen, saliva, menstrual blood, peripheral blood, urine, and sweat, possess evidentiary value and can aid in suspect identification. To enhance the process of trace fluid identification at crime scenes, we aim to devise a methodology utilizing Ultra Performance Liquid Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry (UPLC-MS) to exclusively characterize these bodily fluids.

We collected these different bodily fluids samples from volunteers. After processing the samples, they were analyzed by UPLC-MS. The result will then be analyzed by using standard database to characterize the components.

All the samples had been analyzed by UPLC-MS. However, due to the time limit, this project was then handed to one of my collegues who also studied in ICCAS.

This project is carried out by myself, together with Dr.Yongjian Chen, at Institute of Chemistry, Chinese Academy of Sciences, under the supervision of Fuyi Wang. This project was then passed to Doctor Yiming Zhang at ICCAS.


The Assessment of the Quality of Drinking Water on Different Sources in UCAS

Sep 2018 - Dec 2018

This project is a part of the academic English and scientific skills training session. We chose to assess the drinking water quality from different sources in our campus by analyzing different parameters.

We collected drinking water from different sources and then tested the total concentration of metallic ions, total organic carbon (TOC), total bacterial population and total coliform group. My part is to use ICP-MS to determine the concentration of various metallic ions.

All sources of water in the campus are safe to drink overall. Nevertheless, it is not recommended to drink the barreled water if it is opened for more.

This project is carried out by myself, together with Dr.Yongjian Chen, at Institute of Chemistry, Chinese Academy of Sciences, under the supervision of Fuyi Wang.
